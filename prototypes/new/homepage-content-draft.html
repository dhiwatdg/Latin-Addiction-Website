The questions everyone asks first

Do I really not need a partner?

Nope. Most of our students come solo. We rotate partners throughout the class so everyone gets to dance with everyone. You're welcome to bring a friend, but it's definitely not required.

What should I wear?

Comfortable clothes you can move in — jeans are fine. Wear clean, smooth-soled shoes if you have them (trainers work too, just avoid anything that grips the floor). No special dance shoes needed.

Will it be awkward?

Honestly? The first five minutes might feel a bit new. But the class is structured so you're moving and laughing within minutes. Everyone remembers being a beginner — the room is on your side.

What's the difference between Bachata and Salsa?
SOFTENED: "the easiest Latin dance" changed to "widely considered one of the most beginner-friendly" — fact-check found no source for the superlative claim.

Both are Latin partner dances, but they feel very different. Salsa is faster and more energetic with complex footwork. Bachata is slower, more intimate, built on a simple side-to-side rhythm. It's widely considered one of the most beginner-friendly Latin dances — you can be dancing socially after one class. We teach Bachata exclusively because we believe in doing one thing really well.

Do I need to book in advance?

You can turn up on the night, but we'd love a heads-up. Send us a WhatsApp message and we'll be expecting you — it also means we can answer any questions before you arrive.

How quickly will I be able to dance socially?

Tonight. Seriously. Our beginner class teaches you enough to dance at the social party straight after. It won't be perfect — but it'll be fun, and that's the point.
