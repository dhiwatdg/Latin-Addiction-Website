Your First Night

Your first night,
step by step.

1

Message us on WhatsApp

Just say "Hi, I'd like to try a free class." We'll confirm the next session near you and answer any questions.

2

Arrive at the door

Doors open 15 minutes before class. No booking needed once you've messaged — just walk in. We'll be expecting you.

3

Beginners class (1 hour)

Everything from scratch. You'll learn the basic rhythm, a few moves, and how to lead or follow. We rotate so you practise with different people.

4

Free social dancing

After class, the music gets turned up. Practise what you learned, dance with the regulars, and soak up the vibe. It's free, and it's the best part.

5

Come back (optional £20 pass)

If you're hooked, our 4-Week Newcomer Pass (£20) keeps the momentum going. No pressure — it's there when you're ready.

No booking required — just turn up. We do recommend sending us a quick WhatsApp message first so we know to expect you and can make sure you're in the right place. But there are no reserved spots; every class is walk-in.

Bachata is a Latin partner dance from the Dominican Republic — think sensual movement, rhythmic footwork, and a close connection with your partner. The music is melodic and emotional, and the dance style is warm and expressive. It's one of the fastest-growing social dances in the UK right now.

You can dance at the free social party after your very first class. You won't have every move nailed, but you'll know enough to enjoy yourself. Most people feel comfortable dancing socially after 4–6 weeks of regular classes. The 4-Week Newcomer Pass is designed exactly for this transition.

Our community spans 20s to 60s. There's no typical age — we have university students, professionals, retirees, parents, and everyone in between. The vibe is warm and inclusive. Latin dance has a way of making age completely irrelevant.
